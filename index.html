hopwise: A Python Library for Explainable Recommendation based on Path Reasoning over Knowledge Graphs

Explainability is becoming central to the development of responsible recommender systems, especially as path reasoning over knowledge graphs saw increased adoption for extracting structured, semantic user-item connections. However, reproducible research in such field remains limited due to fragmented implementations, missing utilities, and the lack of standardized evaluation pipelines. In this paper, we propose hopwise, an open-source library that supports the full life-cycle of explainable path reasoning recommendation methods over knowledge graphs, from knowledge graph preparation to explanation path delivery and evaluation. Rather than creating a new library from scratch, hopwise builds upon the modular and widely adopted RecBole ecosystem, enriching it with more knowledge graphs, path sampling utilities, path reasoning methods, and metrics for evaluating explanation path utility, coverage, and diversity. We show the framework's utility by means of a benchmark including two knowledge graphs and several recommendation methods. Code and Data: https://github.com/tail-unica/hopwise.

Abstract

hopwise is a recommendation framework designed to promote the development of explainable-by-design models in a simple and effective manner. Its modular architecture enables flexible control over every component of the model while providing a rich set of functionalities that make it a comprehensive solution for training recommender systems across diverse application settings. In this tutorial we present hopwise, a state-of-the-art recommendation framework that implements state-of-the-art approaches in recommender system explainability. Specifically, this tutorial demonstrates how to leverage Knowledge Graph Embedding methods and state-of-the-art language-model-based approaches, while covering the full pipeline of training, evaluation, hyperparameter optimization, and the generation and management of explanations to be delivered to end users.

Tutorial Outline

  • Introduction to Explainable Recommender Systems (slides, 25min): Literature overview and current advancements in explainable recommender systems, including system user trust, and transparency.
  • Data Requirements (slides, 10min): We'll see how datasets should be preprocessed to be used in an explainable pipeline, ranging from data cleaning to knowledge graph preparation.
  • The Explainable Recommendation Pipeline (hands on, 5min): High-level overview of the explainable recommendation pipeline, showing how to run an easy pipeline via the hopwise API.
  • The modular architecture of hopwise (notebook, 20min): Focus on the modular architecture of hopwise, detailing core components, their communication, and how to extend or replace modules.
  • Path reasoning in Reinforcement Learning (notebook, 15min): Practical aspects of PGPR and CAFE in hopwise, leveraging RL and neural-symbolic reasoning for recommendations and top-k/textual explanations.
  • Path Reasoning on Language Models (slides, 15min): Hands-on with notebooks covering path sampling, tokenization, and model training, highlighting RL vs. LM approaches, constrained generation, top-k, and explanation generation.
  • Challenges and recent advancements (slides, 10min): Current challenges, recent advancements, and open issues in explainable recommendation using KGs and LMs.

Authors

Ludovico Boratto (webpage) is an Assistant Professor at the Department of Mathematics and Computer Science of the University of Cagliari (Italy). His research interests focus on recommender systems and their impact on stakeholders. He has co-authored over 60 papers published in top-tier conference proceedings and journals. His research brought him to give talks and tutorials at top-tier research centres and conferences, including CIKM, UMAP, RecSys, ICDE, ECIR, WSDM, ICDM, DSAA, and ECAI. He is the editor of the book "Group Recommender Systems: An Introduction" by Springer. He is an editorial board member of the "Information Processing & Management" journal (Elsevier) and "Journal of Intelligent Information Systems" (Springer), and guest editor of several journals' special issues. He is part of the program committees of the main Web conferences, where he received four outstanding contribution awards. In 2012, he got his Ph.D. at the University of Cagliari. From May 2016 to April 2021, he joined Eurecat as a Senior Research Scientist in the Data Science and Big Data Analytics research group. In 2010 and 2014, he visited Yahoo! Research in Barcelona.

Gianni Fenu (webpage) is Full Professor at the Department of Mathematics and Computer Science of the University of Cagliari (Italy). His research interests focus on responsible recommender systems, digital education, and personalization. He has authored more than 120 papers in conferences and journals. He has been the scientific responsible a wide range of projects, such as ILEARNTV MIUR-UE (2014-2017, 10 ME, 6 partners) and the European Research M-Commerce and Development.

Mirko Marras (webpage) is an Assistant Professor at the Department of Mathematics and Computer Science of the University of Cagliari (Italy). Before that, he was a postdoctoral researcher at EPFL (Switzerland) and a visiting scholar at Eurecat (Spain) and New York University (USA). His research ranges across various domains impacted by user modeling and personalization. He has co-authored more than 80 papers in top-tier conferences and journals and given tutorials at ECML-PKDD, RecSys, ICDE, ECIR, WSDM, ICDM, and UMAP. He has also co-chaired workshops on related themes at ECIR, WSDM, ICCV, EDM, and ECML-PKDD. He is part of the program committees of top-tier conferences, where he received three outstanding reviewer awards. He is an associate editor for Springer's Journal of Ambient Intelligence and Humanized Computing and Neural Processing Letters.

Francesca Maridina Malloci (webpage) is Assistant Professor at the Department of Mathematics and Computer Science of the University of Cagliari (Italy). Her research interests are focused on predictive analytics, with several papers published in international journal and conference proceedings. Current research activities delved into decision-making algorithms for multi-stakeholders contexts, with attention to recommender systems and education. She received the BSc (2016) and MSc (2018) degrees in Computer Science, both cum laude, from the University of Cagliari. In 2019, she has been visiting scientist at the EURECAT Technology Centre (Spain), collaborating with the Data Science and Big Data Analytics Unit.

Giacomo Medda (webpage) is a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Department of Mathematics and Computer Science of the University of Cagliari, where he received his PhD in Mathematics and Computer Science with honors in 2024. His research spans recommender systems, responsible AI, graph modeling, and generative AI. He has published in top-tier conferences and journals across these areas. He co-edited the special issue "Knowledge Discovery from Graphs" in the Springer Data Mining and Knowledge Discovery journal. He served as an organizer and as a program committee member in several core venues in his field, including UMAP, RecSys, and SIGIR. He co-organized the IRonGraphs workshop at ECIR 2024.

Alessandro Soccol (webpage) is a Research Associate at the Department of Mathematics and Computer Science of the University of Cagliari (Italy). His research interests focus on Explainable Recommender Systems, Personalization and Uncertainty Quantification in Recommender Systems. He has authored works in top-tier conferences such as CIKM and RecSys.
